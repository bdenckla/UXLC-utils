Deuteronomy 5:18.2

The UXLC follows the LC manuscript in placing the siluq (= Unicode meteg) before the etnachta.

The ordering of the marks on the aleph in the LC itself is incorrect.

The word occurs in the Deuteronomy Decalogue and has markings for Lower and Upper cantillation systems. With these dual markings the first (right-to-left) marking is for the Lower system, the second is for the Upper system. In the Upper system, the verse contains only two verses and the word is final.

The siluq is for the Upper system because the chanted verse ends at this word. The expected order of accents is etnachta then siluq. The correct ordering appears in the word's parallel in Ex 20:14 in the LC and UXLC.
