notes:noteThe manuscript’s pointed ketiv (MPK) is מִדָּֽנִאֵ֑ׄאֿל. (We use אׄ (א with an extraordinary upper dot) to hold marks not associated with a parent letter.)
notes:note-2The MPK has no letter to carry a dagesh for the qere’s yod.
notes:note-3The rafeh on the א makes it clear that the qere’s yod functions as a consonant not a vowel, i.e. the qere’s syllables are מִ דָּֽ נִ יֵּ֑אל.
